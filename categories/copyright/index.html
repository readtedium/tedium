Today in Tedium: In losing Prince Rogers Nelson, we’re losing a lot more than a idiosyncratic pop star who defined an entire New Power Generation. We’re losing the last link between our current remix-heavy culture (which, let’s face it, this newsletter wouldn’t exist without) and an era when ownership of creative work was treated as an end-all-be-all. Here was a guy who started the internet era sporting a name that literally could not be searched, who forced lawsuits over barely-noticeable renditions of his songs in YouTube clips, and who made a lot of creative stuff that nobody will likely ever see.
